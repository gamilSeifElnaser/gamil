What is Artificial Intelligence?




Artificial intelligence, more commonly called AI, is technically described as any task performed by a program or a machine, that if a human carried out the same activity, we would say the human had to apply intelligence to accomplish the task.
To put this simply, any machine that does a job that would require some intelligence of a human, is considered AI. This covers a range of machinery and programs including simple things like autodial and more complicated things like computers or robotic machinery.
AI systems will often show the following behaviors to truly be called AI: Planning, learning, reasoning, problem solving, knowledge representation, motion, perception, and manipulation. To some extent, social intelligence and creativity are also included.
Artificial intelligence began long before it was actually possible. Starting with myths, stories, and rumors of artificial beings that had intelligence and consciousness given by master craftsmen.
The seeds of modern AI were then planted by philosophers who compared the human mind and ability to think to mechanical manipulation of symbols. This is like recognizing certain letters make certain sounds and combine to create a specific word. This work and thoughts came together in the programmable digital computer in the 40s.
The focus was on mathematical reasoning, though the purpose was to build a mechanical brain. When presented at Dartmouth College in 1956, those in attendance became leaders in the AI field. A great deal of funding was given, but the project took too long and funding was stopped in the early 70s.
AI again gained interest in the first decades of the 21st century when machine learning was successfully applied to academic problems using computer hardware. Now AI is everywhere from Google pop-up ads to predictive text on your phone and robotic arms that weld in large shops.
As mentioned, AI is now an every day expectation. Though the term AI has lost some meaning due to overuse, it is said to power everything from your toothbrush to your television. AI is being used for nearly every aspect of our lives, good and bad.
As long as AI is used for good, it can continue to allow us to advance and grow, but a single person with a warped idea could bring a huge downfall to society. This is not to suggest this will happen anytime soon, nor that it will ever happen at all, but to offer a warning. AI will continue to evolve, we must evolve with it in order to stay in charge of our own lives.
